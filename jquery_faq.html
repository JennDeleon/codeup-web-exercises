10 National Parks urban legends and scary facts

Grand Canyon National Park
Serial Slayer Robert Spangler took his fourth life there
The Great Sand Dunes National Park
Over 60 UFO sitings, cow mutations and portals to another universe reported
At Mammoth Cave National Park
Sounds of coughing from TB patients who perished at a nearby failed TB hospital
The Norton Creek Trail in the Great Smoky Mountains
Was home to a witch who'd disguise herself as an old woman, snatch children, and use her obsidian stone finger to cut out their livers, which she considered a delicacy.
New Jersey Pinelands National Reserve
The Jersey Devil can be heard screaming at night—though it’s much better to hear him than to see him. The creature is said to have the head of a dog, bat wings, horns and forked tail.
Grouse Lake in Yosemite National Park
The cries of a Native American boy who drowned in the lake calls out to hikers for help
Gettysburg National Military Park
A barefoot ghost known as the “Tennessean” or “The Hippie” will point toward the Plum Run stream and say, “What you’re looking for is over there,” before disappearing.
Crater Lake
Many sightings of Sasquatch have been documented in the area.
Old Faithful in Yellowstone National Park
A bride who was decapitated on her honeymoon now strolls around with her head tucked under her arm
Hawaii Volcanoes National Park
Pele—the volcano goddess inflicts severe punishment on anyone who steals from her by taking souvenirs in the form of volcanic rocks.
